Senior Policy Associate

Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab (J-PAL) Bhubaneswar, ODISHA
Location ODISHA
Date posted 19th January, 2024
Apply by 6th February, 2024

About the Organization

The Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab (J-PAL) is a global research center working to reduce poverty by ensuring that policy is informed by scientific evidence. Anchored by a network of more than 900 researchers at universities around the world, J-PAL conducts randomized impact evaluations to answer critical questions in the fight against poverty.

Job Description

J The Policy team at J-PAL South Asia (J-PAL SA) at the Institute for Financial and Management Research (IFMR) disseminates findings of J-PAL’s research; engages with governments, civil society, and academic stakeholders; catalyses scale-ups and replications of effective projects; and assists in conceptualising new research projects. The team works closely with the global J-PAL office based at MIT to align the regional strategy with broader organisational objectives. In line with its mission to create a long-lasting culture of evidence-informed decision-making, over the past decade, J-PAL SA has continuously developed and refined a model of engaging with state governments in India which ensures that we continue to build on evidence generation through rigorous research, and most importantly, enable the translation of research into action.

To deepen and expand our institutional partnerships with state governments, J-PAL South Asia is setting up an embedded team of highly-motivated individuals, passionate about development research and evidence-based policy making. J-PAL South Asia seeks a Senior Policy Associate to be a part of a team that will have the opportunity to work across multiple, high-stakes state government partnerships, starting with our ongoing engagement with the Government of Odisha and the Government of Punjab. While the position will be based in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, the Senior Policy Associate will be working across both states to support outreach, stakeholder management, policy-research dialogues and policy gap assessments.

Job Responsibilities

  • Supporting the Institutionalisation of an evidence-based approach to policy: The Senior Policy Associate hired for this position will work closely on J-PAL SA’s strategic partnerships with the Government of Odisha and the Government of Punjab, with the aim of institutionalising a data and evidence-informed approach to policy decisions.

This would include:

  • Coordinating and communicating with senior state-level officials to support the generation, dissemination, synthesis and scale-up of rigorous evidence-based policies in the states
  • Working closely with line-departments to identify and conduct policy gap analyses around key development priorities, including scoping interviews with key officials and evidence review and synthesis, and compiling scoping reports.
  • Conducting desk research to prepare policy landscape notes on the state’s priorities, schemes and new developments.
  • Drafting policy-research memos, proposals, presentations, and working closely with the J-PAL SA’s policy and research teams to support these activities.
  • Supporting senior team members, including the state partnership managers, in preparation for government meetings, such as slide-deck preparation, writing notes, and coordinating with the government
  • Supporting in the development and execution of standard protocols for government partnership management
  • Compiling periodic updates for the government and donors for regular reporting on state partnership activities.
  • Working closely with the J-PAL SA policy team to support the generation, dissemination and synthesis of J-PAL’s evidence across sectors.
  • Assisting J-PAL SA’s policy team in preparing background notes, memos, updates, and presentations for J-PAL SA’s partnerships with state governments in India.
  • Writing about J-PAL’s evaluations in relevant documents with a clear, accurate, and non-technical format for a policy audience.
  • Supporting the J-PAL SA policy team on any other tasks, as needed.

Education Qualification/Required Skills and Experience

Your technical expertise, keen eye for detail, strong communication and organizational skills, passion for translating research into action, and interest in international development make you an ideal candidate for the position of Policy Associate.

  • Education: While candidates with a bachelor’s/master’s degree from different fields can apply, training in statistics is a minimum requirement. Candidates with a master’s degree preferred. A technical understanding of impact evaluations is necessary to ensure that J-PAL’s findings are translated into general policy conclusions in a way that is both simple to follow but also accurate and undistorted. Strong interest in international development policy, South Asia development, and/or program evaluation as demonstrated by coursework and professional experience is required. Strong interest in working with issues that overlap with the two sectors and prior experience in the same will be preferred.
  • Experience: 0-2 years of relevant work experience. You may be a recent graduate with related internships, or you may have previous experience in research and/or public policy roles.
  • Communication: You are detail-oriented and are comfortable with public speaking. You understand how to communicate policy lessons from technical academic work.
  • Attitude: You are willing to learn, and are passionate about international development policy and the rigorous research that informs it. You are self-motivated, work hard, and enjoy working in teams.
