Aangan Trust Kandhamal, Nabarangpur, ODISHA

Data Field Officer
Location- Rajasthan (Karauli, Jaipur), Bihar (Jamui, Khagaria, Bhojpur), Jharkhand (Dumka, Ranchi), Odisha (Kandhamal, Nabarangpur)

Our Vision:
To make child protection everyone's business, so that every child is safe everywhere, everyday.

Our Mission:
We build and strengthen child protection systems to ensure that the most vulnerable children are safe, supported, in school, and assured of a childhood free from trafficking, child marriage, hazardous work, violence and exploitation

Background
Aangan works with children in dangerous situations like child trafficking, child marriage, hazardous work and abuse. Our prevention model targets - hotspots- across the country where children are most at risk, either because these are sites of hazardous child labor or are associated with harmful traditions like child marriage, trafficking and girl child discrimination. In such locations, we work with parents, children, government officials and other crucial stakeholders to build strong care and protection systems that keep children safe.

We are working to build an ecosystem for adolescent girls in the districts where they work, so that they stay in schools, and are safe and supported. If you are excited about building systemic solutions for child protection, YOU are the one we are looking out for. JOIN US!!

II. Position Summary

The Data Field Officer plays a key role in leveraging the power of data to push forth organizational and policy goals for the state. S/he will ensure the accuracy and authenticity of child protection data reports generated and presented to government officials and community influencers. His/her work will have a significant impact on the kind of systemic and policy-level change brought about through Aangan within the child protection sphere at the local district level
The Data Field Officer will be working with community volunteer groups and will ensure that all on-field collected quantitative data from various locations is gathered regularly (and where possible-automatically) and fed into a comprehensive database.
Additionally, the Data Field Officer will support the data collection, research, on-ground mobile app training (if required), and staff coordination activities related to the creation of periodical internal reports, as well as reports to external stakeholders such as the government, partners, funders etc.

III. Key Duties and Responsibilities:

Primary responsibilities include but are not limited to:

  • Field data collection form key program staff and community volunteers
  • Asking probing questions to accurately capture the essence of efforts towards child protection in the assigned geography
  • Data entry in prescribed format to document qualitative and quantitative. Timely data entry to
  • Assist program staff in supporting community volunteers in their efforts to address risks of child harm in their communities
  • Work with K&I Associate to maintain regional databases
  • Engage with the Dashboard and central monitoring system to ensure the system reflects the latest updates from the field.
  • Support K&I Associate in maintaining indicators updated at a weekly level.
  • Support program and K&I with data requirements for reporting, or program reviews. Generate reports from the Dashboard to support requests from the program team on need basis.
  • Provide inputs to improve the monitoring system to ensure close alignment with the ground reality of the program.

IV. Knowledge and Technical Skills

  • Effective communication skills to interact with community volunteers, particularly women and children
  • Basic knowledge of child harms such as child labour, child marriage, sexual abuse, etc. and related laws.
  • Fluency in both English and Hindi is essential.
  • Knowledge of Microsoft Office is mandatory (specially Excel)
  • Decent typing speed.
  • Interest in data management

V. Competencies and Abilities:

  • Communicating effectively: Simple and clear written and oral communication
  • Has experience of working with community members
  • Ability to multitask
  • A team player, efficient at delivering within timelines
  • Open to learning and using new products
  • Willing to travel extensively

VI. Education and Experience:

  • A Bachelor's degree
  • Experience in data management (Collection, entry, verification), field survey
  • 2-3 years community work experience, preferably in the development sector
