Program Officer – Community-Based Resilience Program (CBRP)

Rise Against Hunger India Bhubaneswar, ODISHA
Location ODISHA
Date posted 29th January, 2024
Apply by 10th February, 2024

Position : Program Officer – Community-Based Resilience Program (CBRP)

Report to          : Program Manager - Emergency Response

Location           : Bhubaneswar

Salary range     : 6.5 to 7.5 lakh CTC

About Rise Against Hunger India

Rise Against Hunger India is driven by the vision of a world without hunger.  Registered as a Public Charitable Trust, Rise Against Hunger India started operations in 2015. The mission of the organization is to nourish lives, empower communities, respond to emergencies and grow the movement towards ending hunger.  With the headquarters in Bangalore and Regional Offices in Mumbai, Delhi & Hyderabad, Rise Against Hunger India also works in some of the most remote hard-to-reach locations in Odisha & Jharkhand where Cluster Offices have been set up to work with communities. 

Functional Summary:

Rise Against Hunger India works with vulnerable indigenous communities to be sustainable food secure.  Most of these communities are constantly exposed to climate vulnerabilities and chronic disasters which push them back to situations of further vulnerabilities and food insecurities.  Hence, our work on food security must integrate climate adaptation and coping mechanisms into our community programs and ensure that we are having “climate resilient communities”.

The Program Officer is responsible for coordinating and implementing programs that can help vulnerable communities become better prepared to anticipate, prepare, cope, adapt and mitigate disasters. Thus, the primary responsibility of this position is to take care of community-based preparedness and resilience building measures in Rise Against Hunger India operational areas, as well as in other communities. The incumbent will also assist in responding to relief and rehabilitation efforts as and when required.

Specific Areas of responsibility:

  • Under the guidance of the Program leadership team, design, and plan initiatives to make communities aware of the climate hazards, challenges, risks, and the mechanisms to address those.
  • Work with field teams and partners to train individuals, collectives, and groups for them to prepare for and understand disasters and climate change.
  • Develop community-based adaptation (CBA) program for rural and other vulnerable people to improve their livelihood against the imminent and long-term threat of climate change.
  • Design and implement a Resilience Scorecard using tools such as Community Based Resilience Analysis (CoBRA) to assess, measure and track progress of communities in their journey towards being a ‘resilient community.’
  • Support community groups and Rise Against Hunger India field staff in identifying key stakeholders, networks, actors, and decision makers critical to disaster response and recovery efforts.
  • Mobilize stakeholders - government officials, elected bodies, the private sector, non-profits, civil society – to get their buy-in and their capacity for resilience building in general.
  • Organize and facilitate training and capacity building programs for frontline teams and others on disaster preparedness, climate adaptation and other relevant issues.
  • Collaborate with technical agencies in developing and piloting program models such as climate-resilient agriculture (CRA) or climate-smart agriculture (CSA), regenerative agriculture, climate-adaptive seeds, technology enabled soil, seeds & water management etc.
  • Participate and facilitate Rise Against Hunger India disaster response efforts within the region and elsewhere in the country whenever such a response is mounted.
  • Represent Rise Against Hunger India in interagency groups and forums.
  • Support the Program Manager in developing tools and systems to implement emergency relief projects following Sphere Standards and other Cluster guidelines issued by the relevant government agencies.
  • Assist the Program Manager in coordinating disaster response training for Rise Against Hunger India staff who will be deployed during the disaster relief efforts.
  • Any other related task assigned from time to time by Rise Against Hunger India.

Qualifications and Experience:

  • Post-graduate in any discipline with 5 years of work experience and with a minimum of 3 years’ experience in climate adaptation or resilience work.
  • Significant experience in engaging and working with communities to identify needs, implement programs and services, and build capacity.
  • Experience in operations and management emergency response will be an advantage.
  • Must have prior experience of proposal development, government networking and reporting.
  • Preference will be given to candidates having familiarity with and experience in humanitarian principles, Cluster Approaches in Humanitarian Response, Sendai Framework, UNDRR standards, Sphere Standards etc.

Key Competencies required:

  • Must have field affinity and ground level understanding of disaster and climate change issues in India.
  • Demonstrated ability to run things on the ground in a relatively uncertain and difficult environment.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills, demonstrating influencing, collaboration, and negotiation skills.
  • Must be proficient in independently running technical tools required for data collection, analysis, and reporting.

Position Availability – Immediate

CTC – INR 6.5 to 7.5 Lakh (covers statutory benefits, Insurance)

Email - communications@riseagainsthungerindia.org
